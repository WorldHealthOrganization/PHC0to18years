How to support your child in overcoming
daytime wetting

• Be patient and support your child when wetting occurs.

• Be positive and reassure your child if upset.

• Teach your child a good posture when sitting on the toilet and help your child to relax and take time while urinating.

• Create a schedule for your child to wee every 2–3 hours during the day, even if they do not feel like urinating.

• Give plenty of fluids to your child during the day. Drinking less to reduce the amount of wee makes things worse, because it leads to slow bladder filling and makes it harder to feel the bladder filling up.

• Avoid giving fizzy drinks, chocolate, tea, and other drinks with caffeine.

• Provide additional clothes for school and other activities.

DO NOT punish your child for actions that they cannot control.

DO NOT use nappies if your child feels embarrassed.
