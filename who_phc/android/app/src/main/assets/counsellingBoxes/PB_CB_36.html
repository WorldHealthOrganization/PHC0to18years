Febrile seizures

Febrile seizures are convulsions that happen as a response of the brain to fever. They look serious but stop on their own after a few minutes and do not cause any problems. It does not mean that your child has or will develop epilepsy.

About 1 in every 3 children who have had a febrile seizure will have another one, usually within 6–12 months of the first episode. Most children outgrow having febrile seizures by the age of 5 years.

There is no specific measure to avoid febrile seizure. When your child develops high fever (≥ 39 °C) that causes distress, give paracetamol or ibuprofen.

Take your child to the doctor if your child:

• Has more than one seizure within 24 hours

• Is very sleepy or irritable

• Has severe headache or vomiting.
